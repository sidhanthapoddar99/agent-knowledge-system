## Goal

Write the pattern into the skill so a cold reader finds it, follows it, and is
gated on it — the section itself plus every entry path.

## Approach

Authored directly (no subagents — the content was fully specified by the audit
remedy + the delta analysis + sidhantha's review). `references/design-systems.md`
got the new "Variation-set / options-explorer artifacts" section between the
two-homes doctrine and the conventions doctrine, and the Home-A bullet that the
audit flagged as the per-option-files misreading was corrected at its source
(self-contained-per-option now explicitly scoped to competing *systems*).

## Result

- **Section** (~60 lines): the four defining qualities as requirements —
  labeled options with decision furniture (structure by count: 2–3 stacked,
  4–6 switcher/grid, 7–10 switcher with persisted selection), operable-not-
  mocked, **shared realistic fixture** (the "reviewer squints and sees their
  app" bar — flagged as the quality even good runs miss), and built-for-the-
  decision (comparison table, recommendation, optional vote + rationale +
  copy-out; the decision graduates to notes/comments). Treatment override
  stated: the don't-over-design governor does not apply to the options. Worked
  skeleton included.
- **Hooks:** SKILL.md frontmatter description now triggers on "design options /
  variations to compare or pick from"; new triage row; §0 got a third
  treatment bullet (decision tooling = editorial per option); §1's UI/UX
  doctrine names the variation set as its canonical case; §3 gained gate item
  5 **Operable** ("exercise every option's interactions … a static mock passes
  Styled/Complete/Plausible and still fails the pattern") — the exact
  interaction-blindness the audit proved.
